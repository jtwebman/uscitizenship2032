# Global Citizenship-at-Birth Laws: 30-Country Reference

This is the sourced reference data for the international comparison section of USCitizenship2032.com. Every entry includes the actual law and a link to the official or authoritative legal text.

---

## Key Takeaway

Of 30 countries surveyed, **only 3** (Canada, Brazil, Chile) grant unconditional birthright citizenship. The vast majority require at least one parent to be a citizen or permanent resident — or grant no birthright citizenship at all. The U.S. is an extreme outlier among developed nations.

| System Type | Count | Countries |
|---|---|---|
| Unconditional Jus Soli | 3 | Canada, Brazil, Chile |
| Qualified Jus Soli | 11 | UK, Germany, France, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland, Portugal, Belgium, South Africa, India, Spain |
| Jus Sanguinis (descent only) | 16 | Japan, Switzerland, Israel, Singapore, South Korea, Poland, Hungary, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Netherlands, Austria, Italy |

| **Israel** | Jus sanguinis | Citizenship by birth requires an Israeli citizen parent. The Law of Return grants immigration rights to Jews, but birth on Israeli soil alone does not confer citizenship. | Nationality Law 5712-1952, Section 4 | [gov.il](https://www.gov.il/en/pages/nationality-law) |
| **Singapore** | Jus sanguinis | Born in Singapore after 1965: citizen only if at least one parent is Singaporean. | Constitution of Singapore, Article 122 | [sso.agc.gov.sg](https://sso.agc.gov.sg/act/cons1963) |

## Countries the Left Respects

| Nation | System Type | Rule Summary | Law & Section | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## Notable Trends for Site Messaging

- **Ireland and India** both moved AWAY from unconditional jus soli in recent decades — strong precedent for the U.S. doing the same via amendment.
- **Australia's 10-year auto-upgrade** is almost identical to the proposed U.S. v2.0 vesting model — the strongest direct comparison.
- **France's age-18 deferred citizenship** is a similar "grow up here, become a citizen" model but with a longer wait.
- **Germany only added jus soli in 2000** — proof that mature democracies evolve their citizenship frameworks.
- **Every single Nordic country** (Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland) — often held up as progressive models — uses pure jus sanguinis. No birthright soil citizenship at all.
- **Only 3 of 30 countries** grant unconditional birthright citizenship (Canada, Brazil, Chile). The U.S. is in a very small club.
